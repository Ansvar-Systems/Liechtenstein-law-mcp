## Data Authority and Limitations

### Source Authority

This Tool aggregates data from official Liechtenstein legal databases. However:

| Source Type | Authority Level | Use Case |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **gesetze.li (Liechtenstein Legal Information System)** | Authoritative | Statute text, official collection (primary reference) |
| **Liechtensteinisches Landesgesetzblatt (LGBl.)** | Authoritative | Official gazette, original publication |
| **Community/open-data sources** | Supplementary | Cross-references, metadata (verify independently) |
| **EFTA / EEA databases** | Authoritative for EEA law | EEA Agreement, EU-derived legislation |

**CRITICAL**: Community-maintained resources are **NOT** official government publications. While generally reliable, they may contain transcription errors, outdated information, or incomplete coverage.

### Commercial Legal Databases

For professional legal work, **always cross-check with authoritative sources**. Given Liechtenstein's close legal relationship with Switzerland and Austria, the following may be useful for comparative reference:

- Swisslex (for Swiss law comparison), ALEX — Historische Rechts- und Gesetzestexte Online

These services provide editorial oversight and professional-grade currency guarantees that this Tool **cannot** provide.

## Currency and Staleness

### No Real-Time Updates

- Database updates are **manual** and may lag official publications by **weeks or months**
- Liechtenstein law changes continuously through new statutes (Gesetze), amendments (Novellen), and court decisions (Urteile)
- **Last-updated timestamps** in tool responses indicate data age, but should be treated as unreliable

### Staleness Warnings

The Tool includes staleness warnings when data is >30 days old. **These warnings are not exhaustive**:

- Even "fresh" data may be outdated if recent amendments were published in the Liechtensteinisches Landesgesetzblatt (LGBl.)
- Staleness warnings do not guarantee completeness or accuracy
- Always check official sources for the current legal position (aktuelle Rechtslage)

### Amendments and Repeals

- A provision marked "in force" may have been:
  - Amended (geändert) after the last database update
  - Repealed (aufgehoben) but not yet removed from the database
  - Subject to transitional rules (Übergangsbestimmungen) affecting its applicability
- The Tool does **NOT** automatically track amendments in real-time

## Coverage Gaps

### What's Missing

This Tool does **NOT** include:

1. **Complete case law** -- Coverage of the Supreme Court (Oberster Gerichtshof), Constitutional Court (Staatsgerichtshof), and Administrative Court (Verwaltungsgerichtshof) decisions is limited
2. **Historical statute versions** -- Limited availability of historical provision wording (Fassungsvergleich)
3. **Legal commentary** -- No editorial annotations (Kommentare), academic commentary (Lehre), or practice guides
4. **Unpublished decisions** -- Administrative rulings largely absent
5. **Full legislative history** -- Limited coverage of parliamentary materials (Landtagsprotokolle, Berichte und Anträge der Regierung)

### Impact on Professional Use

These gaps mean:

- **Incomplete Legal Research**: Tool results are inherently incomplete and may miss critical authorities
- **Context Missing**: Without commentary and legislative materials, interpretation may be incorrect
- **EEA/Swiss Law Interface**: Cannot fully assess interactions between Liechtenstein law, EEA obligations, and the Customs Treaty with Switzerland
